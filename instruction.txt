ABOUT US – MCAN EKITI STATE CHAPTER
The Muslim Corpers Association of Nigeria (MCAN), Ekiti State Chapter, is a state branch of the
national Muslim corps members’ body established under the National Youth Service Corps
(NYSC) Scheme.
It operates in alignment with the national MCAN headquarters in Mabushi, Abuja, serving as a
faith-based organization committed to Islamic propagation (Da’wah), welfare support,
leadership development, and community engagement among Muslim corps members in Ekiti
State.
HISTORY AND DEVELOPMENT OF MCAN EKITI
MCAN Ekiti State Chapter traces its roots to a modest gathering of northern Muslim migrants
and local Muslim leaders who, decades ago, sought to establish a sustainable religious and
social support system for Muslims residing in Ekiti State.
What began as informal acts of hospitality, offering shelter, guidance, and spiritual support to
Muslim travelers, students, and corpers away from home, gradually developed into a structured
community organization dedicated to Islamic propagation, welfare support, and social cohesion.
In its early years, the association operated without a permanent office or formal structure. With
support from Islamic organizations such as Ansar-Ud-Deen, temporary spaces were provided at
the Ansar-Ud-Deen Mosque in Atikoko, where meetings and coordination took place.
Despite early challenges such as irregular leadership transitions due to the short NYSC service
cycle and funding, the association gradually evolved into a more organized structure with
improved continuity, documentation, and leadership succession.
OUR PURPOSE AND PHILOSOPHY
MCAN Ekiti was founded with the belief that Muslim corps members and community youths
should not only serve the nation through the National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) scheme but
also serve as ambassadors of faith, character, and community development, ensuring that they
leave every community better than they found it.
AIM AND OBJECTIVES
MCAN Ekiti operates in line with the national objectives of MCAN, which include:
•
 Coordinating Muslim corps members in Ekiti State
•
 Promoting Islamic education and the correct understanding of Islam
•
 Supporting Da’wah activities within host communities
•
 Defending the rights and welfare of Muslim corps members
•
 Collaborating with Islamic and community organizations for development
ACTIVITIES AND PROGRAMMES
MCAN Ekiti participates in and implements MCAN programmes, which include:
Orientation Camp Activities
•
 Identification and mobilization of Muslim corps members
•
 Da’wah lectures and Islamic reminders
•
 Qur’an recitation and memorization sessions
•
 Welfare and support services within the camp
Post-Orientation Activities
•
 Weekly Qur’an and knowledge circles
•
 Da’wah lectures and community outreach
•
 Educational support and tutorial sessions
•
 Community development initiatives
IMPACT AND ACHIEVEMENTS
MCAN Ekiti has contributed significantly to the development of Muslim corps members and the
wider community in Ekiti State through:
•
 Strengthening Islamic identity among corps members
•
 Supporting Da’wah efforts in host communities
•
 Promoting peaceful interfaith coexistence
•
 Mentoring Muslim youths in leadership and moral conduct
•
 Improving welfare coordination for Muslim corps members
The association has also contributed to correcting misconceptions about Islam through
consistent engagement and education in diverse communities.
OUR CHALLENGES
Like the national body, MCAN Ekiti faces challenges in two key areas:
1. Financial Constraints
Limited funding affects the maintenance and expansion of facilities such as lodges, Da’wah
outreach, and welfare support systems.
2. MCAN Lodges
Despite its growth, MCAN continues to face challenges in establishing lodges across the state.
These lodges are essential in providing safe and supportive environments for Muslim corps
members, and expanding them remains a major priority for the Association.
OUR LEGACY & FUTURE
Today, MCAN Ekiti stands as a respected and organized Muslim community institution within the
state.
With strong leadership and active youth participation, MCAN Ekiti is committed to
strengthening unity, deepening Islamic knowledge, and building a legacy of service for future
generations.
